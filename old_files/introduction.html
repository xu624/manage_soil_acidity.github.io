   Soil acidification is generally associated with various conditions restricting crop growth, including a decrease in soil fertility in terms of available phosphorus and base cations such as calcium, magnesium and potassium (Zhang et al., 2019) and the release of toxic aluminium and manganese under strongly acid conditions (Kochian et al., 2004). Furthermore, soil acidification enhances the availability of toxic heavy metals, especially lead and cadmium, leading to an increasingly potential risks of their accumulation in crops and animals affecting food quality and human health through the food chain (Mok et al., 2015; De Vries and McLaughlin, 2013).
